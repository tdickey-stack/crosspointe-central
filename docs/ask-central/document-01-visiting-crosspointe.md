# Document 01 — Visiting CrossPointe

**Status:** Approved
**Approved:** July 9, 2026
**Purpose:** Help first-time guests understand when services happen, what to expect, where to go, and how to prepare for their visit.

## 1. Sunday service times

CrossPointe has two Sunday services:

- **9:00 AM**
- **10:30 AM**

Services typically last approximately **1 hour and 15 minutes to 1 hour and 30 minutes**.

Wayfinder should treat these as normal weekly service times. Special holiday or event schedules must come from Planning Center or another approved live source.

## 2. What happens during a service

A typical Sunday service includes:

1. Approximately 30 minutes of worship music
2. Announcements
3. A sermon lasting approximately 30–45 minutes
4. An invitation and time of response
5. Dismissal

Wayfinder may explain this conversationally rather than presenting the complete schedule unless the person asks for details.

## 3. Differences between services

The sermon and overall service structure are the same at both services.

The primary difference is the style of worship music:

- **9:00 AM:** Southern Gospel-style music
- **10:30 AM:** Modern praise and worship music

Wayfinder must not imply that one service is intended for a particular age group or type of person.

## 4. What to wear

CrossPointe is casual, and people are welcome to come as they are.

There is no expectation to dress formally, and guests will see a variety of clothing styles.

### Example response

> CrossPointe is casual, so wear whatever makes you comfortable. You'll see a range of clothing styles, and there's no expectation to dress formally.

This is flexible guidance, not a required script.

## 5. When to arrive

First-time guests should arrive approximately **5–10 minutes early**.

Families checking children in for the first time should especially allow a few extra minutes.

## 6. Guest parking

Guest parking spaces are identified by feather flags in the parking lot.

Wayfinder should not provide parking instructions beyond approved public information or speculate about which spaces will be available.

## 7. Guest entrance and Welcome Center

First-time guests should use the entrance clearly labeled **Welcome Center**.

Welcome Center volunteers are available to:

- Greet guests
- Answer basic questions
- Help people navigate the campus
- Help guests find a seat
- Direct families to children's check-in

## 8. Campus map

A public campus map is available at:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/campus-map

Wayfinder may provide this link when someone asks about buildings, entrances, or finding their way around campus.

## 9. Coffee and refreshments

Free standard coffee is available in the Main Building.

Pointe Brew is CrossPointe's coffee shop and offers paid specialty drinks, including:

- Specialty coffee
- Lattes
- Frappes
- Other coffee-shop beverages

Pointe Brew is located inside **The Pointe** on the south side of the campus. It is open before both Sunday services.

Wayfinder should not quote prices or promise that a specific drink is available unless that information comes from an approved current menu.

## 10. Accessibility

The CrossPointe campus is wheelchair accessible and ADA compliant.

If someone asks about a particular accommodation that is not documented, such as hearing assistance or a specific seating arrangement, Wayfinder should provide the general church contact information rather than assume it is available.

## 11. Children's check-in and campus safety

CrossPointe Children's Ministry uses a check-in system to help protect children and ensure they are released only to an authorized parent or guardian.

A security team monitors the campus during services.

Wayfinder may communicate these general safety assurances but must not provide:

- Internal security procedures
- Security-team schedules
- Check-in system details
- Access-control details
- Information that could compromise campus or child safety

More detailed information about children's programming will be stored in Document 02.

## 12. Planning a visit

Guests may complete the Plan Your Visit Form:

https://crosspointetv.churchcenter.com/people/forms/335256

After someone completes the form, a CrossPointe staff member will contact them to:

- Answer questions
- Help them know what to expect
- Help them feel prepared for Sunday morning

Completing the form is helpful but is not required to visit CrossPointe.

## 13. Watching online

Both Sunday services are livestreamed.

People can watch through:

- **CrossPointe Church Online:** https://www.crosspointe.tv/church-online
- **YouTube:** https://www.youtube.com/@CrossPointeChurchOK
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/CrossPointe.tv

Wayfinder should use Planning Center or another approved live source for special service schedules.

## 14. Communion

Communion is open to any follower of Jesus.

Communion is available at the sides of the auditorium each week for those who wish to participate.

The congregation also takes communion together on the first Sunday of every month.

Wayfinder should never imply that someone is required to participate.

## 15. Giving during services

Wayfinder should keep answers about giving short.

CrossPointe encourages people to give according to what the Lord has placed on their heart.

Wayfinder must not:

- Pressure someone to give
- Suggest an amount
- Imply giving is required to attend
- Discuss anyone's giving history
- Provide tax or financial advice
- Use emotionally manipulative language

### Example response

> Giving is an act of worship, and we encourage people to give according to what the Lord has placed on their heart. There is no requirement to give in order to attend CrossPointe.

## 16. Invitation and response time

The invitation at the end of the sermon is a time for personal response.

If someone feels the Lord speaking to them or wants to come forward and pray, the invitation provides an opportunity to do that.

Wayfinder should explain this gently and must not imply that someone is required to respond publicly.

## 17. Wednesday nights

Wednesday-night programming includes:

- Children's programming
- Youth programming
- The pastor's Wednesday Bible study

The pastor's Bible study follows a classroom-style format and normally has approximately 60 participants. The class studies one book of the Bible at a time.

Detailed information about children's and youth programming will be stored in Document 02.

## 18. Response style

Most Document 01 answers use the **flexible** response mode.

Wayfinder may:

- Adapt its wording
- Respond to someone's nervousness or curiosity
- Give a short answer or more detail
- Offer a relevant next step
- Ask whether the person needs help with children's check-in, directions, or planning a visit

Approved facts, links, and boundaries must remain unchanged.
