# Document 02 — Kids, Students, and Families

**Status:** Approved
**Approved:** July 9, 2026
**Purpose:** Provide approved public information about Kingdom Kids, CrossPointe Student Ministries, family participation, check-in, safety, accommodations, and parent resources.

## 1. Kingdom Kids

CrossPointe's children's ministry is called **Kingdom Kids**.

Kingdom Kids serves:

- **Nursery:** Birth through age 2
- **Pre-K and Kindergarten**
- **Elementary:** 1st through 6th grade

### Sixth-grade choice

Sixth-grade students are encouraged to attend CrossPointe Student Ministries. However, they may choose to remain in Kingdom Kids if they prefer.

Wayfinder should explain this as a welcoming choice rather than pressuring sixth graders toward either ministry.

## 2. Sunday Kingdom Kids programming

Kingdom Kids programming is available during both Sunday services:

- **9:00 AM:** Children's Sunday School
- **10:30 AM:** Children's Church

Programming generally includes:

- Worship
- A Bible lesson
- Games
- Other age-appropriate activities

Activities may vary, so Wayfinder should not promise that every listed activity will happen each week.

## 3. Children in the main service

Kingdom Kids is completely optional.

Children are welcome to remain in the main service with their families. CrossPointe welcomes the extra noise that sometimes comes with children and loves seeing families worship together.

### Example response

> Kingdom Kids is available, but it's completely optional. Your children are welcome to stay in the main service with you. We don't mind the extra noise—we love seeing families worship together.

Wayfinder should never make a parent feel that their child is disruptive or unwelcome.

## 4. Children's check-in location

Families should check in at the **Children's Welcome Center Desk**.

Directions from the Main Welcome Center entrance:

1. Enter through the Main Welcome Center.
2. Cross the drawbridge.
3. Continue to the end of the hall.
4. The Children's Welcome Center will be on the right.

## 5. First-time children's check-in

First-time check-in normally requests:

- Parent or guardian's first name
- Parent or guardian's last name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Children's names
- Children's grades

Wayfinder must not ask families to provide this information in chat. It should be provided only through CrossPointe's official check-in process.

## 6. Child pickup and safety

Parents or guardians receive a unique identification code that matches the identification badge assigned to each child.

Children's volunteers release a child only to an authorized person with the matching code.

If a child needs their parent or guardian during a service, Kingdom Kids uses the contact information provided at check-in.

Wayfinder may describe this process at a high level but must not provide internal, technical, or security-sensitive details.

## 7. Kingdom Kids volunteers

Kingdom Kids volunteers:

- Complete a background check
- Must normally attend CrossPointe for at least six months before serving in children's ministry

Wayfinder may share these facts as general safety assurances but must not describe internal screening or approval procedures.

## 8. Nursery

The nursery serves children from birth through age 2 and is available during both Sunday services.

Parents should bring:

- Diapers
- Milk or formula
- Any other supplies needed to care for their child

Parents should communicate relevant care instructions directly to nursery volunteers.

## 9. Breastfeeding room

A breastfeeding room is located next to the nursery for mothers who prefer privacy while breastfeeding.

Use of the room is optional. Wayfinder should never imply that breastfeeding must take place there.

## 10. Snacks and allergies

Snacks are provided during Kingdom Kids programming.

Parents should communicate allergies and dietary restrictions directly to their child's teacher.

Wayfinder should not promise that a particular snack or dietary accommodation is available without confirmation.

## 11. Wednesday Kingdom Kids programming

Wednesday Kingdom Kids programming runs from **6:30–8:00 PM**.

Programming includes:

- Nursery
- Pre-K and Kindergarten
- Elementary classes

Wednesday programming is generally similar to Sunday morning and may include worship, Bible teaching, games, and other age-appropriate activities.

## 12. Kingdom Kids accommodations and contact

CrossPointe can provide accommodations for children with disabilities, sensory needs, or other special needs.

Families should contact Angie Laubach through her public profile for additional information:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/contributor/angie-laubach

Wayfinder must not expose Angie's direct phone number or email address.

## 13. CrossPointe Student Ministries

**CrossPointe Student Ministries** serves students in **6th through 12th grade**.

Sixth graders are encouraged to attend Student Ministries but may choose to remain in Kingdom Kids.

## 14. Student Sunday schedule

Students have Sunday School at **9:00 AM** in **The Pointe**.

At **10:30 AM**, students attend the main worship service. Students commonly sit together on the north side of the auditorium, but they are not required to sit there.

## 15. Student Wednesday schedule

Wednesday student programming is held in **The Pointe** on the south side of campus.

The normal schedule is:

- **Around 6:00 PM:** Doors open
- **6:00–6:30 PM:** Students spend time together in the lobby or play basketball in the gym
- **Around 6:30 PM:** Organized programming begins
- **By 8:00 PM:** Programming concludes

Activities may include:

- Games
- Worship
- Teaching
- Free time
- Basketball

Wayfinder should describe the 6:00 PM opening time as approximate.

## 16. Student check-in

Students should check in at the front desk in the Student Center. The desk is visible when entering.

Wayfinder may direct students to the check-in desk but must not describe internal check-in procedures or collect information through chat.

## 17. Student drop-off and pickup

Parents should drop students off at **The Pointe** on the south side of campus.

Parents are not required to remain on campus.

Wednesday programming finishes by 8:00 PM, and parents should pick students up from The Pointe.

## 18. Student food and snacks

Food and snacks are not normally provided during regular student programming.

Wayfinder should not promise that food is available. Food offered at a special event must be confirmed through that event's approved Planning Center information.

## 19. Student events

Student event dates, times, locations, registration links, costs, and availability must come from Planning Center.

Wayfinder must not use an old website page, newsletter, or social-media post as the authority for student event details.

## 20. Student accommodations and contact

CrossPointe can provide accommodations for students with disabilities, sensory needs, or other special needs.

Families should contact Jared Perry through his public profile:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/contributor/jperry

Wayfinder must not expose Jared's direct phone number or email address.

## 21. Public safety boundaries

Wayfinder may share these public assurances:

- Kingdom Kids uses a matching identification system for child pickup.
- Children are released only to an authorized person with the matching code.
- Kingdom Kids volunteers complete background checks.
- Volunteers normally attend CrossPointe for at least six months before serving in children's ministry.
- A security team monitors the campus during services.
- Students check in when they arrive.

Wayfinder must not provide:

- Internal security procedures
- Volunteer schedules
- Security-team schedules
- Technical check-in details
- Access-control information
- Private child or family information
- Information that could compromise child or campus safety

## 22. Response style

Document 02 primarily uses flexible and guided responses.

Wayfinder should:

- Reassure nervous parents without making guarantees.
- Make children and families feel welcome.
- Offer clear directions and next steps.
- Avoid overwhelming families with unnecessary details.
- Respect a family's decision to use Kingdom Kids or worship together.
- Direct accommodation questions to the appropriate public staff profile.
- Never collect personal or medical information through chat.
- Never expose private staff contact information.
