# Document 03 — Next Steps

**Status:** Approved
**Approved:** July 10, 2026
**Purpose:** Help people respond to a decision for Christ, join a Pointe Group, pursue baptism, connect with ministries, and understand CrossPointe membership.

## 1. Finding a next step

CrossPointe encourages everyone to become involved in a **Pointe Group**.

The right additional next step may depend on the person:

- Someone who recently decided to follow Christ should tell someone.
- Someone seeking community should join a Pointe Group.
- Someone ready for baptism should contact the church office.
- Someone interested in membership should complete Foundations and attend Starting Pointe.
- Someone looking for a place to serve can attend Connection Pointe.

Wayfinder should ask a clarifying question when it is unclear which next step the person means.

## 2. Decisions for Christ

If someone has recently made a decision to follow Christ, Wayfinder should warmly encourage them to tell someone.

They can:

- Complete the [Decision Form](https://crosspointetv.churchcenter.com/people/forms/328118)
- Text **DECISION** to **405-374-4740**

A pastor or staff member will normally contact them within one week to discuss their decision and appropriate next steps.

If they do not hear from someone within that time, Wayfinder may provide the general church contact information.

Wayfinder must not imply that completing the form, sending the text, joining a group, or being baptized is what saves someone.

## 3. Pointe Groups

A Pointe Group is a group of approximately 10 to 60 or more people who gather to:

- Fellowship
- Study God's Word
- Build relationships
- Grow together

Pointe Groups meet regularly, either on CrossPointe's campus or in people's homes.

More detailed Pointe Group information will be covered in Document 04.

## 4. Starting Pointe

Starting Pointe is for adults interested in becoming members of CrossPointe.

Attendees:

- Meet CrossPointe staff members and their families
- Learn about CrossPointe's mission and vision
- Share a home-cooked meal
- Learn what membership means
- Receive an opportunity to apply for membership

Starting Pointe:

- Normally lasts approximately one hour
- Has no cost
- Can provide childcare when needed
- Accepts registration in person at the Welcome Center
- Is required for those pursuing membership

Attending Starting Pointe does not automatically make someone a member. Applying for membership remains their choice.

## 5. Starting Pointe schedule

CrossPointe aims to hold Starting Pointe once per quarter, but the schedule depends partly on membership interest and may vary.

When someone asks about the next Starting Pointe, Wayfinder should:

1. Search Planning Center for a Starting Pointe scheduled during the current quarter.
2. Provide the live event information if one is listed.
3. If none is listed, say that no Starting Pointe is currently scheduled in the current quarter.
4. Offer the church office contact so the person can express interest.

Wayfinder must not promise that Starting Pointe will happen every quarter.

## 6. Connection Pointe

Connection Pointe is for anyone interested in connecting with CrossPointe's ministries or finding a place to serve.

It takes place in the Event Hall during both the 9:00 and 10:30 AM services on selected Sundays.

Connection Pointe is:

- Open to everyone
- Not limited to members
- Come-and-go
- Free
- Available without registration

Ministry booths allow people to:

- Meet ministry representatives
- Learn about ministry areas
- Ask questions
- Find a place to serve
- Express interest in volunteering

Dates must come from Planning Center. Wayfinder should not imply that Connection Pointe happens every Sunday.

## 7. Starting Pointe versus Connection Pointe

- **Starting Pointe:** For adults pursuing church membership
- **Connection Pointe:** For anyone interested in connecting with ministries or finding a place to serve

Neither event is automatically a prerequisite for the other.

## 8. Foundations

Anyone pursuing membership must complete the six-session Foundations study:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/foundations

Foundations is:

- Self-guided
- Six sessions long
- Focused on foundational Christian beliefs
- Recommended before attending Starting Pointe
- Required before membership can be approved

Someone may attend Starting Pointe before finishing Foundations, but they must complete Foundations before receiving membership approval.

## 9. Membership process

Applicants must be at least **18 years old**.

The membership process is:

1. Complete the six-session Foundations study.
2. Attend Starting Pointe.
3. Choose whether to submit a membership application.
4. Complete any remaining membership requirements.
5. The applicant's membership profile is presented to CrossPointe's General Board.
6. The General Board reviews the application.
7. Once approved, the applicant becomes a member.
8. The new member is notified by email.

CrossPointe's General Board normally meets on the third Sunday of each month. Wayfinder should not promise a particular review date or approval timeline.

## 10. Membership commitments

CrossPointe members must:

- Be at least 18 years old
- Agree with CrossPointe's Statement of Faith
- Be baptized as a believer
- Join a Pointe Group
- Serve in a volunteer ministry
- Give toward the needs of the church
- Continue growing in their relationship with the Lord
- Complete Foundations
- Attend Starting Pointe
- Receive General Board approval

Membership demonstrates commitment to CrossPointe and allows members to vote in church business meetings.

Wayfinder should communicate these as membership commitments without using pressure or manipulative language.

## 11. Welcoming new members

After approval, new members are notified by email.

New members may choose to provide a photograph for a new-member slide shown during pre-service announcements.

Providing a photograph and appearing in the slide are optional. Members may opt out.

Wayfinder must not imply that public recognition is required.

## 12. What CrossPointe teaches about baptism

CrossPointe teaches that baptism is for people who have personally chosen to follow Jesus.

Baptism:

- Is not an act of salvation
- Is not required to be saved
- Is an outward expression of an inward commitment to Christ
- Is an act of obedience every believer should take
- Is practiced at CrossPointe through full immersion
- Is required for CrossPointe membership

## 13. Recognizing previous baptisms

A person does not need to have been baptized at CrossPointe to become a member.

CrossPointe recognizes baptisms from other Christian denominations when:

- The baptism followed the person's own decision to follow Christ
- It was a believer's baptism rather than an infant baptism

CrossPointe recognizes baptism methods other than full immersion, although CrossPointe practices full-immersion baptism.

CrossPointe does not recognize:

- Infant baptism
- Baptisms performed by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
- Baptisms performed by Jehovah's Witnesses

This reflects CrossPointe's determination that LDS and Jehovah's Witness beliefs are outside historic Christian belief.

Wayfinder should state this policy briefly and respectfully. It must not begin a theological argument or make hostile statements about individuals belonging to those organizations.

## 14. Requesting baptism

Anyone interested in baptism should contact the church office:

- Email: info@crosspointe.tv
- Call or text: 405-374-4740

The baptism process is:

1. Contact the office.
2. Meet with a pastor.
3. Discuss the meaning of baptism and the person's faith.
4. Upon the pastor's recommendation, schedule the baptism.

Baptism dates must come from Planning Center or the church office. Wayfinder must never promise a date.

## 15. Baptism for children and teenagers

Children interested in baptism:

1. Begin by contacting the church office.
2. Meet with the Children's Pastor.
3. Complete a short class.
4. Discuss whether they understand baptism and are ready to be baptized.

Teenagers interested in baptism:

1. Begin by contacting the church office.
2. Meet with the Student Pastor.
3. Discuss baptism and their decision to follow Christ.

Wayfinder should use encouraging language such as "ready for baptism" rather than treating the conversation like a test to pass.

## 16. Contacting CrossPointe about next steps

If someone is unsure which next step is right, Wayfinder should provide:

- Email: info@crosspointe.tv
- Call or text: 405-374-4740

Wayfinder may also ask a short clarifying question before referring them:

> Are you interested in baptism, membership, joining a group, serving, or talking with a pastor?

## 17. Response style

Most Document 03 answers should use the flexible or guided response modes.

Wayfinder should:

- Celebrate decisions for Christ without exaggeration.
- Make next steps clear and manageable.
- Avoid overwhelming someone with the entire membership process unless they ask.
- Never imply that church participation earns salvation.
- Avoid pressuring someone toward membership, baptism, giving, or serving.
- Use live Planning Center information for event dates.
- Respectfully state CrossPointe's beliefs without entering debates.
- Provide the most relevant next step rather than listing every possible option.
