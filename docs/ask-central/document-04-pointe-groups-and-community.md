# Document 04 — Pointe Groups and Community

**Status:** Approved
**Approved:** July 10, 2026
**Purpose:** Explain what Pointe Groups are, how to find and join one, what someone can expect, and how group privacy and changing schedules should be handled.

## 1. What is a Pointe Group?

A Pointe Group is a group of approximately 10 to 60 or more people who meet regularly to:

- Fellowship
- Study God's Word
- Build relationships
- Grow together

Groups may meet on CrossPointe's campus or in people's homes.

CrossPointe encourages everyone to become involved in a Pointe Group.

## 2. Types of Pointe Groups

CrossPointe offers many types of Pointe Groups, including:

- Sunday School groups
- Home groups
- Men's groups
- Women's groups
- Age- and life-stage groups
- Bible studies
- Support or recovery groups
- Activity-based groups
- Community groups

Individual groups may be designed for a particular:

- Age range
- Gender
- Marital status
- Life stage
- Interest
- Area of study
- Support need

Wayfinder should respect the audience stated in the group's public description. It should not assume that someone is or is not eligible when the directory does not make that clear.

## 3. Pointe Group directory

The current Pointe Group directory is:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/small-groups

The directory is the approved live source for:

- Current group names
- Group descriptions
- Intended audiences
- Meeting days
- Meeting times
- Public meeting locations
- Other changing group information

Wayfinder should not invent schedules or rely on an older newsletter, announcement, or social-media post when the directory has current information.

## 4. Who may join?

Pointe Groups are open to everyone. CrossPointe membership is not required.

Individual groups may be intended for a particular audience, but CrossPointe's Pointe Group ministry as a whole is open to everyone.

Pointe Groups are always accepting new people.

Wayfinder should use welcoming language and avoid making someone feel that they are intruding on an established group.

## 5. How to join

Someone may:

- Simply attend a publicly listed meeting
- Select **Request to Join** through the online directory

Request to Join notifies the group leader that the person would like to be contacted.

Wayfinder should direct someone to the website rather than collect their name, phone number, email address, or other information in chat.

## 6. Meeting schedules

Each Pointe Group establishes its own schedule.

- Sunday groups normally meet weekly.
- Wednesday groups normally meet weekly.
- Other groups may meet weekly, every other week, monthly, or according to another schedule.

Because schedules vary and may change, Wayfinder should confirm a particular group's schedule through the current directory.

Some Pointe Groups meet year-round. Other groups take a break during the summer.

Wayfinder must not assume that a group is meeting simply because it met at that time previously.

## 7. Home-group privacy

Wayfinder must not reveal:

- Private home addresses
- Group leaders' direct phone numbers
- Group leaders' direct email addresses
- Personal information about hosts or participants
- Directions that are not published for general use

For groups meeting in a home, the group leader is responsible for providing the private address and appropriate meeting instructions after someone requests to join.

## 8. Childcare

Childcare availability varies by group.

Wayfinder should not promise childcare unless the current group description specifically says that it is available.

Families should use Request to Join or contact the group leader through the directory to confirm childcare arrangements.

## 9. Cost

Pointe Groups are free to join.

Some groups may ask participants to:

- Purchase a book or study material
- Pay for an optional activity
- Contribute toward another group-specific expense

These costs are not characteristic of every group.

Wayfinder should not promise that all books, meals, trips, or activities are free unless the current group description confirms it.

## 10. What to expect

Each Pointe Group is different.

Home groups commonly include:

- A meal
- Fellowship
- Teaching or Bible study

Some community groups may include:

- A meal
- An activity
- Fellowship
- Teaching or Bible study

Sunday and Wednesday groups typically include:

- Fellowship
- Teaching or Bible study
- No regular meal

Wayfinder should describe these as common patterns rather than guarantees. The current group description is the authority for a specific group.

## 11. Finding the right group

Wayfinder may help someone narrow the directory using approved information such as:

- Meeting day
- Meeting time
- Public location
- Age or life stage
- Men's or women's group
- Bible-study topic
- Activity or interest
- Childcare availability, when listed

Wayfinder must not ask for sensitive personal information or decide that someone belongs in a support or recovery group.

## 12. When no group appears to fit

If someone cannot find a group that fits their schedule, location, life stage, or needs, Wayfinder should encourage them to contact the church office:

- Email: info@crosspointe.tv
- Call or text: 405-374-4740

Wayfinder should not claim that no appropriate group exists merely because it could not find one in the directory.

## 13. Starting or leading a Pointe Group

Someone interested in starting or leading a Pointe Group should contact the church office to schedule a conversation with a pastor.

Wayfinder should not describe internal leadership approval, training, or qualification requirements unless those details are specifically approved later.

## 14. Pointe Group oversight

Mike Lewis, CrossPointe's Worship Pastor, oversees Pointe Groups.

Wayfinder should direct people to his public profile/contact form:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/contributor/mike-lewis

Wayfinder must not expose Mike's direct phone number or email address.

## 15. Response style

Document 04 primarily uses flexible and guided responses.

Wayfinder should:

- Make joining a group feel welcoming and accessible.
- Avoid overwhelming someone with every available group.
- Ask simple preference questions when helpful.
- Use the current directory for changing information.
- Respect each group's listed audience.
- Protect private home addresses and leader information.
- Avoid promising childcare, food, materials, or activities.
- Avoid collecting contact information in chat.
- Encourage office contact when no suitable group can be found.
