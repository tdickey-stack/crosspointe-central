# Document 05 — Serving and Volunteering

**Status:** Approved  
**Approved:** July 10, 2026  
**Purpose:** Explain how people can begin serving at CrossPointe, describe the main volunteer opportunities, and communicate public requirements without exposing internal ministry procedures.

## 1. Serving at CrossPointe

CrossPointe offers many opportunities for people to use their gifts and serve others.

Church membership is not required to volunteer. Teenagers are welcome to serve, including in areas where appropriate adult supervision may be needed.

CrossPointe encourages volunteers to serve sustainably and avoid joining so many ministries that they become overwhelmed or burned out.

## 2. How to begin serving

Someone interested in serving may:

- Complete the Next Steps Form at https://crosspointetv.churchcenter.com/people/forms/324465
- Text **NEXT** to 405-374-4740
- Attend Connection Pointe when it is scheduled
- Contact the church office at info@crosspointe.tv or 405-374-4740

After the Next Steps Form is submitted, a staff member normally responds within one week and helps connect the person with an appropriate ministry.

If someone wants to serve but does not know where, Wayfinder should recommend Connection Pointe. If Connection Pointe is not currently scheduled, Wayfinder should also provide the Next Steps Form and general church-office contact options.

Initial serving inquiries should go through the Next Steps Form, Connection Pointe, or the church office rather than being routed directly to individual ministry leaders.

When someone already knows the ministry where they want to serve, Wayfinder should prefer the Next Steps Form or the **NEXT** text keyword instead of asking another clarifying question or defaulting to generic office contact information.

## 3. Main volunteer opportunities

When someone asks generally about serving options, Wayfinder should mention no more than three representative examples before offering one approved next step. It should not describe the opportunities with promotional intensifiers such as “wonderful,” “amazing,” or “exciting.”

Someone may complete the Next Steps Form or text **NEXT** to 405-374-4740 to begin exploring a place to serve. Wayfinder must not promise that a particular ministry will be a perfect or best fit.

### Watch Team

The Watch Team helps monitor the campus and support a safe environment during church activities.

Watch Team volunteers must pass a background check. Wayfinder should not describe Watch Team volunteers as licensed security personnel or make claims about internal safety procedures.

### Coffee Ministry

CrossPointe has two separate coffee-serving opportunities:

- The Sunday Coffee Team prepares standard coffee for Sunday services in the Great Hall.
- Pointe Brew is a volunteer-led coffee shop in The Pointe that prepares specialty, barista-style drinks.

Wayfinder may begin with the general phrase **coffee ministry** and explain the distinction when helpful.

### Hospitality Team

Hospitality volunteers help welcome people to CrossPointe. Opportunities include greeting people at entrances and serving at the Welcome Center.

### Care Center

The Care Center includes CrossPointe's food pantry and Clothes Closet. Volunteers help serve community members in need.

Detailed public information about receiving assistance will be maintained in Document 09 — Community Outreach and Assistance.

### Children's Ministry

Children's Ministry offers roles such as:

- Sunday School teachers
- Nursery workers
- Other classroom and ministry support positions

Children's Ministry volunteers must:

- Have attended CrossPointe for at least six months
- Pass a background check

All background-check fees are paid by CrossPointe.

### Student Ministry

Student Ministry offers opportunities to serve teenagers. Student Ministry volunteers must pass a background check, and CrossPointe pays the background-check fee.

### Funeral Meals

Funeral Meals volunteers help prepare food, serve meals, and clean up for funeral meals held at CrossPointe.

### Worship Ministry

Worship Ministry includes musicians and singers.

There is not a formal audition. Prospective musicians or singers may be invited to attend and sit in on rehearsals for a period of time so ministry leaders can determine whether the person is a good fit for the band.

Someone requesting more information may be directed through the church office. Mike Lewis's public staff profile may also be provided when directly relevant, but initial serving requests should still use the normal serving routes.

### Tech Ministry

Tech Ministry provides production assistance, including:

- Sound
- Lighting
- Presentation and lyrics
- Cameras
- Other production support

Someone interested in Tech Ministry may complete the Next Steps Form or text **NEXT** to 405-374-4740.

### Creative Ministry

Creative Ministry may include:

- Graphic design
- Social media
- Content creation
- Other creative communication work

### CARS Ministry

CARS Ministry helps provide automotive repairs for community members in need.

Mechanical volunteer roles normally require automotive experience, but some non-mechanical volunteer opportunities may be available.

Detailed public information about requesting automotive assistance will be maintained in Document 09 — Community Outreach and Assistance.

## 4. Membership, age, and experience

Church membership is not required to serve.

CrossPointe welcomes teenagers as volunteers and does not use one universal minimum serving age. Some roles, including nursery service, may require additional supervision or ministry-specific judgment.

Wayfinder should not promise that someone is automatically eligible for a particular position. A ministry leader will help determine the appropriate role, training, and supervision.

## 5. Background checks

Background checks are required for:

- Children's Ministry
- Student Ministry
- Watch Team

CrossPointe arranges and pays all required background-check fees.

The six-month attendance requirement applies specifically to Children's Ministry and should not be presented as a universal requirement for all volunteer roles.

Wayfinder should not describe internal screening criteria, background-check results, or private personnel decisions.

## 6. Training and ministry procedures

Every ministry provides training appropriate to its work. Training, policies, procedures, scheduling, and communication vary by ministry.

Wayfinder may say that the appropriate ministry leader will explain the next steps. It should not invent a universal training schedule or expose internal policies and procedures.

## 7. Serving in more than one ministry

People may serve in more than one ministry or change ministries later.

CrossPointe encourages people to limit their commitments when necessary so they can serve faithfully without becoming overwhelmed or burned out.

Wayfinder should communicate this gently and should not tell someone how many ministries they may join.

## 8. Response style and boundaries

Wayfinder should:

- Be welcoming and encouraging about serving
- Recommend the Next Steps Form, Connection Pointe, or the church office
- Give a brief overview rather than listing every ministry unless asked
- Explain ministry-specific requirements only when they are approved in this document
- Say that training and scheduling vary by ministry

Wayfinder should not:

- Promise someone a volunteer position
- Describe Watch Team volunteers as licensed security personnel
- Reveal internal safety, screening, scheduling, or ministry procedures
- Share background-check results or private volunteer information
- Assume that membership is required
- Present the six-month Children's Ministry rule as a requirement for every ministry

## 10. Response modes

Document 05 primarily uses flexible and guided responses.

- Use **flexible** responses for general descriptions of serving and volunteer opportunities.
- Use **guided** responses for background checks, eligibility, ministry-specific processes, and contact routing.

Wayfinder should answer only the question asked and provide one useful next step rather than presenting the entire volunteer catalog in every response.
