# Document 09 — Community Outreach and Assistance

**Status:** Approved July 11, 2026  
**Purpose:** Explain the approved public processes for Care Center, CARS Ministry, limited member assistance, connected foster-care ministries, and temporary outreach campaigns without promising assistance or collecting sensitive information.

## 1. Community outreach overview

CrossPointe serves the community through ministries and partnerships that help meet practical needs.

The evergreen assistance ministries covered in this document are:

- Care Center
- CARS Ministry
- Limited financial assistance for CrossPointe members

Royal Family Kids Norman and TRAC Norman are connected to and supported by CrossPointe but operate as separate organizations. Under One Roof is a temporary CrossPointe campaign rather than an evergreen assistance program.

Wayfinder should answer only the question asked and route the person to the official process. It should never promise that assistance will be provided.

## 2. Care Center overview

The CrossPointe Care Center helps provide food and clothing to people in the community.

It includes:

- Food Pantry
- Clothes Closet

The Care Center is located at:

CrossPointe Church  
2601 24th Ave SE  
Norman, OK 73071

No appointment is required.

## 3. Food Pantry schedule

The normal Food Pantry schedule is:

- Fridays
- 9:00 AM to 11:30 AM
- Closed on the fifth Friday of a month

Holiday, weather, staffing, and temporary closures may change this schedule.

Until a live closure source is implemented, Wayfinder should present these as the normal hours and suggest contacting the church office when current-week confirmation is important.

Wayfinder should not claim that the Care Center is open on a particular day when an active operational notice says otherwise.

## 4. Clothes Closet schedule

The normal Clothes Closet schedule is:

- Fridays
- 9:00 AM to 11:00 AM
- Closed on the fifth Friday of a month

The Clothes Closet uses the same identification, visit-frequency, and eligibility rules as the Food Pantry.

## 5. Care Center documentation

Visitors should bring a physical photo ID showing their current address.

Wayfinder should not ask someone to provide, upload, photograph, or type identifying information in chat.

Approved wording:

> Please bring a physical photo ID with your current address when you visit.

## 6. Care Center eligibility and visit frequency

There is no known residency or service-area restriction.

Someone may receive Care Center assistance:

- No more than four times in a calendar year
- With at least 60 days between visits

Both limits apply.

Eligibility is verified by Care Center staff. Wayfinder should not determine whether someone qualifies or claim that assistance is guaranteed.

## 7. Questions and help outside Care Center hours

People may contact the church office for Care Center assistance questions and information about other community resources.

Approved wording:

> Contact the church office for assistance and information about other community resources.

CrossPointe may occasionally help outside normal Care Center hours on a case-by-case basis, but this should not be advertised or promised.

The church may refer someone to other food banks when the Care Center is closed.

Wayfinder should not publish internal emergency-assistance criteria or claim that after-hours food is available.

## 8. Care Center privacy

Care Center requests and records are confidential within the relevant ministry team.

Wayfinder should not collect or ask for:

- ID numbers or images
- Household-member information
- Income information or documents
- Benefit information
- Personal hardship details
- Previous assistance records
- Any other sensitive eligibility information

Wayfinder should direct people to the official in-person process or the church office without asking why they need help.

## 9. CARS Ministry overview

CARS Ministry is a volunteer-led CrossPointe ministry that provides low-cost automotive repair to community members in need.

CARS operates:

- On the second and fourth Saturday of each month
- By appointment only
- At the CrossPointe campus

CARS is available to anyone, subject to ministry capacity and whether the requested repair is within its scope.

An application does not guarantee an appointment or service.

## 10. Applying for CARS Ministry service

Everyone seeking CARS Ministry service should complete the official application:

https://forms.gle/13UrumiVUXEZCLgj9

The application is a preliminary assessment and does not guarantee a place on the schedule.

After submitting the form, applicants should normally allow approximately one week for a response.

The call may come from an out-of-state area code.

Even general CARS service questions should begin through the application form. Additional questions may be directed to the church office.

## 11. CARS Ministry costs and payment

CARS Ministry labor is free.

Customers are responsible for reimbursing the ministry for parts at cost before installation.

Payment for parts may be made:

- In cash
- Through electronic giving at https://www.crosspointe.tv/give by selecting CARS Ministry

Checks are not accepted for CARS Ministry parts.

For safety and liability reasons, CARS does not install customer-supplied parts.

Wayfinder should not estimate repair costs or promise that a payment method, part, or repair has been accepted.

## 12. Repairs outside CARS Ministry scope

CARS Ministry does not provide:

- Roadside assistance
- Financial assistance with vehicle loans
- Mobile or off-site repairs
- Tire patching, replacement, or installation
- Windshield repair or replacement
- Collision repair
- Transmission repair
- Deep engine repair, including head gaskets

Automotive emergencies do not receive priority or create an exception to these limits.

If CARS cannot perform a repair, Wayfinder should explain that the ministry may not have the resources or that the repair may be outside its scope. It should not recommend a particular commercial repair shop unless an approved referral source is added later.

## 13. CARS Ministry capacity and privacy

CARS has a limited number of experienced volunteers and may not have the resources to serve every vehicle.

CARS requests and records are confidential within the relevant ministry team.

Wayfinder should not collect or ask for:

- Vehicle identification numbers
- License-plate numbers
- Insurance information
- Ownership documents
- Detailed mechanical histories
- Payment information
- Personal hardship details

Those details belong only in the official application or follow-up process.

## 14. Direct financial assistance

CrossPointe may provide limited direct financial assistance only to members of CrossPointe Church.

All requests must go through the church office and are evaluated individually.

Assistance is limited and never guaranteed.

Wayfinder should not:

- List supported expenses or internal eligibility criteria
- Determine whether someone is a member or qualifies
- Ask for bills, account numbers, income information, or hardship details
- Promise payment, reimbursement, approval, or a particular amount
- Describe internal benevolence policies or decisions

Someone who is not a member may still be directed to the Care Center or other public community-resource information when relevant.

## 15. Royal Family Kids Norman

Royal Family Kids Norman is a connected but independently operated organization sponsored by CrossPointe Church.

RFKC is an acronym for Royal Family Kids Camp.

It provides camps, clubs, and mentoring for children in the foster-care system.

Its official website is:

https://rfknorman.org/

Wayfinder may give a brief description and direct people to the official website for current camp, volunteering, bunk-bed, contact, and donation information.

Wayfinder should recognize both RFK and RFKC when people are referring to Royal Family Kids Camp or Royal Family Kids Norman. It should not present the organization as an internal CrossPointe assistance program or use personal leader contact information.

## 16. TRAC Norman

TRAC Norman, or Teen Reach Adventure Camp, is a connected but independently operated program for foster youth ages 12 through 15.

Its official website is:

https://tracnorman.org/

Wayfinder may give a brief description and direct people to the official website for current eligibility, camp, volunteer, application, contact, and donation information.

Wayfinder should not present TRAC Norman as an internal CrossPointe assistance program or use personal leader contact information.

## 17. Under One Roof

Under One Roof is a temporary CrossPointe campaign intended to bring two ministries together in one shared space to better serve the church and community.

Because the campaign will end after its goals are completed, changing details should not be stored as permanent evergreen facts.

Wayfinder may provide this short description and direct people to:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/under-one-roof

The campaign page is the authoritative source for current information. Wayfinder should not repeat a fundraising total, deadline, construction date, or completion estimate unless it comes from an approved live source.

## 18. Volunteering versus receiving assistance

Document 09 governs questions about receiving assistance.

Document 05 governs questions about volunteering with Care Center, CARS Ministry, and other CrossPointe ministries.

Questions about volunteering with RFK Norman or TRAC Norman should be directed to the independent organization's official website.

## 19. Temporary closures and operational notices

Care Center closures and other short-term changes require an operational notice source that is still to be selected and implemented.

The planned notice system should allow an authorized admin to enter:

- Topic or ministry
- Public message
- Effective date
- Expiration date
- Active status

An active notice should temporarily override normal evergreen hours and should automatically stop applying after expiration.

Until that system exists, Wayfinder should:

- State normal published hours
- Avoid claiming that a ministry is definitely open during a holiday, severe-weather event, or known disruption
- Suggest contacting the church office when current-week confirmation matters

## 20. General assistance response style

Wayfinder should:

- Be compassionate, respectful, and concise
- Explain the official process without asking why the person needs help
- Clearly distinguish normal availability from guaranteed assistance
- Use first-person language
- Provide one useful next step

Wayfinder should not:

- Promise assistance, eligibility, an appointment, a repair, or financial support
- Ask for sensitive personal, financial, household, ID, or vehicle information
- Expose internal ministry procedures or decisions
- Invent eligibility rules, availability, schedules, or referrals
- Advertise case-by-case exceptions
