# Document 12 — Campus Navigation and Facilities

**Status:** Approved July 11, 2026  
**Purpose:** Help guests find public buildings, entrances, rooms, parking, ministries, and accessible routes on the CrossPointe campus without exposing security-sensitive or private facility information.

## 1. Campus overview

CrossPointe Church is located at:

2601 24th Ave SE  
Norman, OK 73071

The main public areas are:

- Main Building
- Children's Center
- The Pointe
- Care Center
- CARS Ministry garage

The online campus map is:

https://www.crosspointe.tv/campus-map

Wayfinder may provide the map with written directions. When a map label differs from this approved document, Wayfinder should use the current preferred wording in this document.

## 2. Main Building

The building labeled North Building on the older campus map is normally called the Main Building.

The Main Building includes:

- Great Hall
- Welcome Center
- Sanctuary
- Event Hall
- Adult classrooms upstairs
- Church offices
- Standard Sunday coffee area
- Public restrooms
- Connection to the Children's Center

Wayfinder should prefer Main Building while recognizing North Building as an older map label or alternate description.

## 3. Sanctuary and Auditorium

The room labeled Sanctuary on the campus map is CrossPointe's main worship space.

CrossPointe normally calls it the Sanctuary. Auditorium may also be recognized as referring to the same room.

From the Welcome Center entrance:

1. Enter the Great Hall.
2. Turn left into the hallway leading to the Sanctuary.

Accessible parking is available near the north entrance to the Sanctuary.

Wayfinder should not describe the Sanctuary's north entrance as the primary first-time guest entrance unless accessibility or another specific need makes it the most useful route.

## 4. Welcome Center entrance and guest parking

The Welcome Center entrance is beside the guest parking area shown near the Great Hall on the campus map.

Guest parking is marked by feather flags.

After entering, the Welcome Center desk is immediately visible in the Great Hall. Volunteers are available during services to greet guests, answer questions, and help them find a room or seat.

This is the recommended entrance for most first-time guests who are not checking children in.

## 5. Great Hall

The Great Hall is the large main lobby entered through the Welcome Center entrance.

It contains or provides access to:

- Welcome Center desk
- Standard Sunday coffee
- Sanctuary hallway
- Event Hall
- Children's Center
- Adult classroom elevator
- Hallway to the church offices

The Great Hall is a useful starting point for public directions inside the Main Building.

## 6. Event Hall

The Event Hall is on the first floor of the Main Building.

From the Welcome Center entrance:

1. Enter the Great Hall.
2. Continue straight down the Great Hall.
3. Enter the Event Hall from the Great Hall.

Wayfinder may use Planning Center's approved room name and event information to confirm that a particular event is scheduled there.

## 7. Church offices

The church offices are in the Main Building.

During office hours, public visitors may use the separate office entrance shown on the campus map.

Office hours are Monday through Thursday, 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM, subject to holiday or temporary closures. Offices are normally closed Friday through Sunday.

During Sunday services, from the Welcome Center entrance:

1. Enter the Great Hall.
2. Walk approximately halfway down the Great Hall.
3. Turn right into the office hallway.
4. Continue to the end of that hallway.
5. Turn right to reach the office area.

Wayfinder should not provide directions into staff-only workspaces or restricted office areas.

## 8. Children's Center

The Children's Center is the current name of the castle-themed children's building or wing connected to the Main Building.

Kingdom Kids is the name of the children's ministry. Children's Center is the name of the physical area.

The Children's Center includes:

- Children's Welcome Center and check-in
- Nursery
- Preschool area
- Elementary area upstairs
- Children's Center elevator and stairs
- Public restrooms
- Access to the outdoor playground

## 9. First-time family entrance and parking

The Children's Center entrance is normally open for Sunday check-in and is the recommended entrance for first-time families bringing children.

The Children's Center has its own nearby guest parking on the north side of the parking lot. Accessible parking is also available in the Children's Center parking area.

Families may also enter through the main Welcome Center entrance. From there:

1. Continue straight down the Great Hall.
2. Pass across the actual drawbridge in the castle-themed hallway.
3. Continue to the end of the hall.
4. The Children's Welcome Center and check-in area will be on the right.

Wayfinder may use the word drawbridge because it is an actual, recognizable campus landmark.

## 10. Nursery, preschool, and breastfeeding room

The nursery and preschool areas are on the first floor of the Children's Center.

The breastfeeding room is across the hall from the nursery. It is available for mothers who prefer privacy, but its use is optional.

Wayfinder may direct families to the Children's Welcome Center for help locating a particular nursery or preschool room.

## 11. The Palace and elementary ministry

The Palace is the current name of the upstairs elementary area in the Children's Center.

It serves elementary-aged children.

The Children's Center elevator and stairs serve the upstairs Children's Center and The Palace. This upstairs area is separate from the upstairs Adult Classrooms.

Wayfinder should not direct someone seeking an Adult Classroom to the Children's Center elevator.

## 12. Adult classrooms and elevator

Adult Classrooms are upstairs in the Main Building and are individually numbered.

From the Welcome Center entrance, the elevator serving the Adult Classrooms is directly to the right after entering the Great Hall.

Signs upstairs direct people to individual classroom numbers.

If someone asks for a classroom number not included in approved live event or group information, Wayfinder should direct them to follow the upstairs signs or ask at the Welcome Center rather than inventing a turn-by-turn route.

## 13. Elevators and stairs

Both public elevator and stair areas shown on the campus map are available to the public.

- The elevator directly to the right after entering the Great Hall serves the upstairs Adult Classrooms.
- The elevator inside the Children's Center serves the upstairs Children's Center and The Palace.

The two upstairs areas are separate.

Wayfinder should provide the elevator route when someone requests an accessible route and should never assume a person can use stairs.

## 14. Public restrooms

Public restrooms are available:

- In the hallway outside the Sanctuary
- In the hallway leading to the church offices
- In the Children's Center

Restrooms are clearly marked with wayfinding signs inside the building.

If the person's exact location is unclear, Wayfinder should recommend following the nearest restroom signs or asking a Welcome Center volunteer.

## 15. Outdoor playground

The outdoor playground is beside the Children's Center and has an exterior gate.

Wayfinder may describe its general location but should not provide gate, access-control, supervision, or child-security instructions.

Questions about playground access during children's programming should go to Children's Ministry staff or the Children's Welcome Center.

## 16. Care Center

The Care Center is a separate building and is not internally connected to the Main Building.

Care Center visitors should:

1. Park in the large parking lot between the Main Building and The Pointe.
2. Use the clearly labeled Care Center entrance.

Care Center hours, eligibility, and assistance rules are governed by Document 09.

Wayfinder should not direct Care Center visitors through the Main Building.

## 17. The Pointe

The Pointe is on the south side of the CrossPointe campus and requires an outdoor walk from the Main Building.

The Pointe includes:

- Main lobby
- Pointe Brew
- Student Center
- Game Room
- Classrooms
- Community Center
- Gymnasium
- Weight Room
- Community Center Office

The Pointe and Main Building are not connected by an indoor hallway.

## 18. The Pointe entrance

The main Pointe entrance opens into the shared lobby for Pointe Brew and the Student Center.

Student Ministry check-in is at the front desk immediately after entering The Pointe.

The main Pointe entrance is wheelchair accessible.

## 19. Pointe Brew

Pointe Brew is directly in the lobby inside The Pointe.

It also has a separate exterior entrance marked by a Pointe Brew sign above the door.

The separate Pointe Brew entrance is not wheelchair accessible. Anyone needing an accessible route should use the main Pointe entrance and reach Pointe Brew through the lobby.

Wayfinder should distinguish Pointe Brew's specialty coffee from the standard Sunday coffee served in the Great Hall.

## 20. Student Center

Student Center is the preferred current name for the room labeled Student Sanctuary on the older campus map.

It is directly connected to The Pointe lobby.

Student check-in is at the front desk immediately inside The Pointe entrance.

Wayfinder should use Student Center while recognizing Student Sanctuary as an older map label.

## 21. The Pointe classrooms

The Pointe classrooms are in the back of the building.

From The Pointe entrance:

1. Enter the lobby.
2. Go through the Game Room.
3. Continue through the double set of glass doors.
4. Enter the classroom hallway.

Wayfinder should rely on approved room names, numbers, and on-site signs for a specific classroom rather than inventing a room location.

## 22. Community Center, gymnasium, and weight room

Community Center refers to the gymnasium and weight-room side of The Pointe rather than only the gym itself.

From The Pointe entrance, the gymnasium may be reached by either of these public routes:

- Continue past the Student Center entrance, travel through the connecting hallway, and enter through the gym door.
- Go through the Game Room and glass doors into the classroom hallway, then use the gym door at the end of that hallway.

The Community Center includes the gymnasium, Weight Room, and Community Center Office.

Wayfinder should provide general public directions without describing keys, locked doors, staff access, equipment controls, or security procedures.

## 23. CARS Ministry garage

CARS Ministry operates from a separate garage building east of The Pointe.

Someone with a confirmed CARS appointment may be directed to the CARS garage and should follow the arrival instructions provided by CARS Ministry.

Someone without an appointment should not be directed to arrive at the garage for service. They should use the official CARS application process in Document 09.

## 24. Parking

Public parking is available around the Main Building and The Pointe.

Approved guest-parking information includes:

- Guest parking near the Welcome Center entrance, marked by feather flags
- Guest parking near the Children's Center entrance for first-time families
- Accessible parking near the north entrance to the Sanctuary
- Accessible parking in the Children's Center parking area

Wayfinder should not promise that a particular parking space will be available.

## 25. Accessibility

The CrossPointe campus is wheelchair accessible and ADA compliant.

All public entrances shown on the campus map are wheelchair accessible except the separate exterior Pointe Brew entrance beneath the Pointe Brew sign.

For accessible directions, Wayfinder should:

- Recommend the main Pointe entrance for Pointe Brew
- Use the correct public elevator for upstairs destinations
- Mention the accessible parking closest to the relevant destination when known
- Avoid suggesting stairs as the only route

Wayfinder should not claim that an unknown temporary obstruction, construction condition, or equipment outage does not exist. Current accessibility concerns should be directed to the church office.

## 26. Getting help when lost

During Sunday services, someone who is lost should visit the Welcome Center in the Great Hall. Welcome Center volunteers can help them find a room or destination.

At other times, the person may contact the church office:

- Email: info@crosspointe.tv
- Call or text: 405-374-4740

Wayfinder should ask for the person's current public landmark and destination when a short clarifying question would make the directions safer or easier to follow.

Wayfinder should not request a live location, track someone, or ask them to share personal location data.

## 27. Live event room information

When an event question includes a room or location, live Planning Center data and Central's approved room-renaming rules determine the current public room name.

Document 12 may then provide directions to that approved room.

Wayfinder must not use the campus map to assume that a particular event is scheduled in a room.

## 28. Safety and privacy boundaries

The provided campus map is general and does not expose restricted areas. Wayfinder may give directions to its public destinations.

Wayfinder must not provide:

- Keys, codes, credentials, or access-control instructions
- Alarm, camera, security, or Watch Team positions
- Staff-only routes or private workspaces
- Network, A/V, utility, mechanical, or infrastructure details
- Child checkout or security workarounds
- Instructions for entering locked buildings or rooms
- Claims about whether a building, room, or staff member is currently unattended

If a door is locked or a route is unavailable, Wayfinder should direct the person to the Welcome Center or church office rather than suggest an alternate unauthorized entrance.

## 29. Response style

Wayfinder should:

- Use current preferred room and building names
- Recognize older map labels as aliases
- Give short landmark-based directions
- Start from the entrance or landmark the person is actually using
- Mention outdoor travel between the Main Building and The Pointe
- Offer an accessible route when relevant
- Link to the campus map when helpful

Wayfinder should not:

- Give a long campus tour when a person asks for one room
- Invent a room number or turn
- Assume a person is starting at the Welcome Center
- Hide the fact that a route requires going outside
- Direct a walk-in visitor to the CARS garage without an appointment
- Treat the separate Pointe Brew entrance as wheelchair accessible

## 30. Approved aliases

Wayfinder should recognize these equivalent or historical labels:

- Main Building = North Building on the older map
- Sanctuary = Auditorium
- Student Center = Student Sanctuary on the older map
- Children's Center = physical children's building or area
- Kingdom Kids = children's ministry, not the building name
- Community Center = gymnasium and weight-room side of The Pointe

Wayfinder should prefer the first current term in each applicable answer.
